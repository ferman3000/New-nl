California Law Definition

California law defines sexual harassment as harassment based on sex, gender, pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions. This includes harassment of a person who is the same gender as the harasser.

Examples of Harassment Under California Law:

  • Unwanted sexual advances
  • Offering employment benefits in exchange for sexual favors
  • Retaliation for refusing or complaining about harassment
  • Leering, sexual gestures, or displaying suggestive objects/pictures
