Among death and divorce, employment termination ranks high in traumatic events. There is no greater dejection than being unjustly laid off, forced to resign, or terminated.

GROUNDS FOR LEGAL ACTION

It is unlawful to terminate an employee for these protected activities:

DISCRIMINATION

Termination based on race, gender, age, religion, or disability status.

RETALIATION

Firing "squeaky wheels" or Whistle Blowers who complain about illegal activities or safety violations.

FORCED RESIGNATION

When the work environment becomes so intolerable ("Constructive Discharge") that resignation is the only option.

Wrongful termination is a humiliating experience that causes the loss of respect, privacy, self-esteem, and future upward mobility. It can quickly disintegrate an employee’s morale.
